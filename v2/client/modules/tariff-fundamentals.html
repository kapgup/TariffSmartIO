Tariff Fundamentals

A quick interactive introduction to the basic concepts of tariffs and how they work in international trade. Learn about the different types of tariffs and their economic impact in just a few minutes.

What Are Tariffs?

A tariff is a tax imposed by a government on goods and services imported from other countries.

Click to explore the main purposes of tariffs:

  • To protect domestic industries from foreign competition
  • To raise revenue for the government
  • To address trade imbalances between countries
  • As a response to unfair trade practices by other countries

Simple Tariff Example:

Without Tariff

Imported Product Price: $100

No tariff applied

Final Consumer Price: $100

With 25% Tariff

Imported Product Price: $100

25% tariff: +$25

Final Consumer Price: $125

Types of Tariffs:

Ad Valorem Tariff

A percentage of the value of the imported good

Example: 15% of the product's value

Specific Tariff

A fixed fee based on the type of item

Example: $5 per barrel of oil

Compound Tariff

Combines both ad valorem and specific tariffs

Example: 10% + $2 per unit

Reciprocal Tariff

A tariff imposed in response to another country's tariff

Example: Country A imposes 20% on imports from Country B because Country B imposed tariffs on Country A's goods
